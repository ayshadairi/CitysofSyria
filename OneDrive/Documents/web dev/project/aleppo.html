General Information about Aleppo

Aleppo, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, has a rich history spanning thousands of years. Once a major trade hub between the Mediterranean and Mesopotamia, the city has witnessed the rise and fall of empires, from the Roman and Byzantine eras to the Islamic Caliphates.

Known for its historical landmarks, Aleppo is a city that embodies the spirit of Syrian heritage, with its ancient citadel, vibrant souks, and iconic mosques.

Key Facts About Aleppo

Fact Details
Location Northwest Syria, near the Turkish border
Population Approximately 2.3 million people
Significant Landmark Aleppo Citadel, a UNESCO World Heritage site
Historical Importance Was a major trade center in ancient times and a key city in the Silk Road
Modern Significance A symbol of Syria's resilience, despite damage during the Syrian Civil War

Famous Landmarks of Aleppo

  • Aleppo Citadel: A massive fortified palace in the city center with a history dating back to the third millennium BC.
  • The Great Mosque of Aleppo: A historic mosque, known for its beautiful architecture and significant cultural value.
  • Al-Madina Souq: A traditional covered market that has been a trading hub for centuries.
